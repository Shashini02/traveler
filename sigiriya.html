World Heritage Sigiriya

The Sigiriya fortress is an ancient rock structure that can be found in Sri Lanka's Matale District. It is a site of archaeological and historical significance. The fortress is made up of a massive column of rock that is 180 meters tall. Sigiriya is a World Heritage site that was established in the ancient city of Sigiriya. It is regardedas one of the most significant examples of urban planning in the world.

History of Sigiriya

The origin of the Sigiriya rock can be traced back to a feud between two brothers. The fortress was built by Kashyapa to protect himself from his brother, King Mogallana. Unfortunately, this tragic event eventually led to the death of the king.
The heir to the throne was King Kashyapa, but he had taken over after his brother, King Moggallana, was deposed. Because of this, Kashyapa built the fortress to protect himself. He did this to ensure that his brother would not return to reclaim his throne. The fortress was constructed with a mordern technology. The colorful frescod on the walls of the Sigiriya rock are a testimony to the construction of the fortress.
After defeating Kashyapa, the fortress was destroyed. It used to be a Buddhist monastery, but following the palace's destruction, it was turned into a Buddhist monastery. In 1831, the Sigiriya rock was discovered by Europeans. British army officer Jonathan Forbes was on his way to Polonnaruwa when he came across the fortress. Archaeologists and historians worked on the site for a long time, and it was through their excavations that the magnificent features of the fortress were discovered. One can easily see the fortress' various features, such as gardens and parks. The massive rock was constructed with modern technology.

Ideal time to visit Sigiriya

The November to March period is the most popular time for foreign tourists to visit Sri Lanka. During this period, many of them travel to Colombo and other areas. There are also high prices in hotels and accommodations throughout the country. It is recommended to book your hotel rooms early to avoid late fees.
The second tourist season in Sri Lanka begins in April to September and ends in August. During this period,there are various festivals and events in the country, such as the Kandy Esala Perahera and the Katharagama Festival. It is a good time to reserve your hotel accommodations.
